## Overview

Functional Equation, often abbriviated as FE, is a famous topic in math
olympiads nowadays. This page is for those who have no experience with FE's.

## What is functional equation?

Almost 90% of the FE's in olympiad goes as follows:

_Let $A$, $B$ be a set. Find all functions $f\colon A\rightarrow B$ such that
(some equation)_

Here $A$ and $B$ can be anything, but most of the time it's $\mathbb{Z_{>0}}$,
$\mathbb{Z}$, or $\mathbb{R}$.

### common mistake

Note that $f$ can be **_anything_** as long as it's from $A$ to $B$. We
introduce few common pitfalls about the definition of functions:

- $f$ does not need to be a polynomial.
- $f$ does not need to be differentiatable.
- $f$ does not need to be continuous.

So think $f$ as an arrow rather than a manipulation. And indeed there's some
problem that have a ``strange'' solution which seems like it came out of
nowhere. Let us show some example: some (real olympiad!) problem have solutions
with

$$
f(x)=
\begin{cases}
    \text{whatever odd} \qquad \text{ if $x=1$} \\
    1 \qquad \text{$x>1$ odd} \\
    2 \qquad \text{$x>1$ even}
  \end{cases},
$$

$$
f(n) =
  \begin{cases}
  n+1 & n \text{ even} \\
  n+5 & n \equiv 1 \pmod 4 \\
  n-3 & n \equiv 3 \pmod 4.
  \end{cases},
$$

$$
f(x) =
  \begin{cases}
    +1 & x\notin S \\
    -1 & x\in S
  \end{cases}
$$

_where $S$ is any subset of $(-\infty, -\frac 23)$._

These are often called a ``monster FE'', and we'll cover this in another module.
